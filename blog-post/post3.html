Baby Sleep Training Guide: Simple Methods for Better Sleep

Learn simple sleep training methods to help your baby sleep better through the night and establish healthy sleep habits.

Helping your baby learn to sleep well is one of the most important things you can do for your family. Good sleep helps babies grow, learn, and be happy.

When to Start Sleep Training

Most babies are ready for sleep training when they:

  • Are 4-6 months old
  • Can sleep for longer stretches at night
  • No longer need nighttime feedings (check with your doctor)
  • Have a fairly predictable daily pattern

⚠️ Important Note

Always talk to your pediatrician before starting sleep training. Every baby is different, and your doctor can give you personalized advice.

Simple Sleep Training Methods

1. The Gentle Check Method

This method is good for parents who want to comfort their baby while encouraging independent sleep.

How it works:

  • Put baby down awake but sleepy
  • Leave the room
  • If baby cries, wait 3-5 minutes before checking
  • Comfort baby briefly without picking up
  • Gradually increase wait times each night

2. The Fading Method

Very gradual approach that works well for sensitive babies.

How it works:

  • Stay with baby until they fall asleep
  • Each night, move slightly farther away
  • Eventually sit outside the room
  • Minimal talking or touching

3. The Pick Up Put Down Method

Good for younger babies who need more comfort.

How it works:

  • Put baby down awake
  • If they cry, pick them up and comfort them
  • Put them down again once calm
  • Repeat until baby falls asleep

Creating a Good Sleep Environment

The right environment helps any sleep training method work better:

Dark Room

Use blackout curtains to make the room dark

White Noise

Helps block household sounds

Comfortable Temperature

Keep room between 68-72°F (20-22°C)

Safe Sleep Space

Firm mattress, no loose bedding

Baby Sleep by Age

Understanding what's normal for your baby's age helps set realistic expectations:

4-6 Months

  • 10-12 hours at night
  • 3-4 hours in naps
  • 2-3 naps per day

6-12 Months

  • 11-12 hours at night
  • 2-3 hours in naps
  • 2 naps per day

1-2 Years

  • 11-12 hours at night
  • 2-3 hours in naps
  • 1-2 naps per day

Common Sleep Training Challenges

Teething

During teething, you may need to pause sleep training and provide extra comfort.

Illness

Always comfort your baby when they're sick. Resume sleep training when they're healthy.

Travel

Expect some changes after travel. Return to your routine as soon as possible.

Developmental Milestones

Learning new skills like rolling over or crawling can temporarily affect sleep.

Success Tips

  • Choose a method that feels right for your family
  • Be consistent for at least 1-2 weeks
  • Create a calming bedtime routine
  • Use our baby sleep calculator for guidance
  • Be patient - every baby is different

Sleep training is about teaching your baby healthy sleep habits. With patience and consistency, you can help your baby learn to sleep well, which benefits the whole family. Remember that some babies take longer than others, and it's normal to have good nights and challenging nights.
