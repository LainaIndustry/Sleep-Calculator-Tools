Understanding Sleep Cycles

What Are Sleep Cycles?

Sleep cycles typically last about 90 minutes and consist of four stages: N1, N2, N3 (deep sleep), and REM sleep. Waking up at the end of a complete cycle helps you feel more rested.

Why 90 Minutes?

The 90-minute cycle is based on the natural rhythm of our sleep architecture. Completing full cycles ensures you get adequate deep sleep and REM sleep, both crucial for physical and mental restoration.

Fall Asleep Time

Most people take about 15 minutes to fall asleep. Our calculator accounts for this by subtracting 15 minutes from your desired bedtime to ensure you're in bed with enough time to actually fall asleep.
